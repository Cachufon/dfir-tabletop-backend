## 2) Purpose

This tabletop exercise is a **discussion-based simulation** intended to improve incident readiness by testing:
- decision-making under time pressure,
- escalation and authority clarity,
- communications governance (internal and external),
- legal/regulatory awareness (as applicable),
- and identification of actionable improvements.

**This is a no-fault learning exercise.** It is not an audit and does not prove technical control effectiveness.

---

## 3) Rules of engagement

1. **Discussion-based:** No systems are touched; no live technical testing occurs.
2. **State assumptions:** If information is missing, state assumptions and what you would request.
3. **Time-boxed decisions:** The facilitator may force decisions to keep the exercise moving.
4. **One conversation at a time:** Use a “parking lot” for side topics.
5. **Confidentiality:** Treat exercise discussion and outputs as sensitive unless agreed otherwise.

## 8) Exercise structure

Typical structure (adjust to duration):
- Opening & rules (5 minutes)
- Scenario context (5 minutes)
- Inject cycles (majority of session)
- Hotwash / debrief (10–15 minutes)

During injects, participants should answer:
- What decision must be made right now?
- Who owns that decision (role)?
- What is the immediate priority in the next 30 minutes?
- What information is needed, and who requests it?
- What is the business impact of containment vs continued operation?
- What will we communicate internally and externally, and who approves it?

## 10) Discussion questions (optional)

Use these to steer conversation if the group stalls.

### Detection & Awareness
- What signals indicate this may be a real incident?
- What is your initial working hypothesis?
- What evidence would you request first, and why?

### Decision-Making
- What decision must be made now?
- What tradeoffs exist (risk vs business impact)?
- What is the threshold to escalate and/or declare a major incident?

### Escalation & Authority
- Who is incident commander right now?
- Who can authorize disruptive containment actions?
- When does leadership become involved?

### Communication
- Who is the internal source of truth?
- Who can speak externally?
- What is the update cadence and approval path?

### Legal / Regulatory (as applicable)
- What are the notification decision points?
- What is the difference between “suspected” and “confirmed” breach in your workflow?
- How do you preserve documentation and manage privilege boundaries?

### Documentation & Tracking
- How are decisions recorded?
- How are action items owned and time-boxed?
- What is your mechanism to track improvements post-exercise?

---

## 11) Hotwash (debrief)

At the end of the exercise, participants will discuss:
- What worked well?
- What slowed down decision-making?
- Where were decision rights unclear?
- What information/tools were missing?
- What are the top 3 improvements in the next 30–90 days?
