# Compromise Assessment Models

Gruve Compromise Assessments are offered in three models. Models describe HOW we approach the assessment (depth, focus, and driver) and can be combined with one or more modules (Identity, SaaS, Endpoint) to define scope.

## Model 1 — Broad Sweep

A fast, low-friction assessment using a baseline set of detections plus light custom rules. The goal is to quickly detect obvious or likely signs of compromise with minimal effort and minimal disruption.

Typical use cases:
- “We want a periodic compromise check.”
- “We just tightened security controls and want a sanity check.”
- “We are evaluating our overall risk of silent compromise.”

## Model 2 — Post-Incident Sweep

A compromise assessment driven by a specific incident (internal or external). This model uses incident reports, IOCs, and threat intelligence to search for related activity, confirm blast radius, and validate containment.

Typical use cases:
- “We had an incident. Did we fully contain it?”
- “A vendor or IR firm already handled the incident; we want an independent second look.”
- “We know IOCs and TTPs from a breach. Did they show up elsewhere in our environment?”

## Model 3 — Targeted Threat Hunt

A tailored, scenario-based assessment focusing on a specific threat actor, campaign, TTP cluster, or industry vertical. This model is proactive and hypothesis-driven.

Typical use cases:
- “We saw Threat Group X in the news. Are they in our environment?”
- “We have threat intel from our sector; can you hunt for it?”
- “We want a focused hunt around a known attack path or scenario.”

## Combining Models and Modules

Models describe the approach. Modules (Identity, SaaS, Endpoint) describe the surface area. Any model can be combined with any module(s), depending on customer data availability and needs.

For example:
- Broad Sweep + Identity only
- Broad Sweep + Identity + SaaS
- Post-Incident Sweep + SaaS + Endpoint
- Targeted Threat Hunt + Identity + SaaS + Endpoint

We scope and price engagements based on the model and selected modules.
